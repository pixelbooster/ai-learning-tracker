Rules that make the difference

  • 1
    Consistency beats intensity. Two hours every week beats eight hours once a month. Programming and mathematics require repetition spaced over time to actually stick.
  • 2
    Never copy-paste code. Always type examples yourself. The friction of typing forces your brain to slow down and process the syntax.
  • 3
    Write things down by hand. Sketch diagrams on paper. Physical writing builds intuition that reading alone never does.
  • 4
    Sit with confusion rather than skipping it. Every concept in Phase 1 is load-bearing. Something unclear in week 3 compounds into a wall in week 20.
  • 5
    If you miss a week, just continue. Pick up exactly where you left off. Missing one week changes nothing over a multi-month timeline.
  • 6
    Build things, don't just watch. The milestone projects are not optional extras — they are the proof that learning happened.
